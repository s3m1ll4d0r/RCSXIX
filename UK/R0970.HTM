   FOR 6 SERVINGS

1 Beef tongue

250 cc of oil

250 cc of white wine

1 tablespoon of breadcrumbs

4 cloves

1 orange peel

1 laurel leaf

1 lemon leaf

1 beef bouillon cube

1 teaspoon of sugar

Black pepper

Salt

Wash the tongue very well and scrape or brush the outer skin. Cover with cold water, add a bouillon cube and cook for one hour (1/2 hour if you use a pressure cooker). Let the tongue cool in the water, drain and peel off the outer skin with a sharp knife. Heat the oil. Fry the tongue, turning it several times. Add the wine. When it begins to boil reduce the flame and add the breadcrumbs, crushed cloves, sugar, laurel leaf, lemon, the orange peel, cut in thin strips, and salt and pepper to your taste. Cook at a low flame for 1 1/2 hours. Stick a fork in the end of the tongue to check if it is tender enough.
