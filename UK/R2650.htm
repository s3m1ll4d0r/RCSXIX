Double Boiled Pudding

   FOR 6 SERVINGS

1 small loaf of sliced bread.

4 eggs.

125 cc of milk.

125 cc of rum.

50 g of Corinth raisins.

300 g of sugar.

Butter for the bread.

With the sugar and a glass of water make a syrup to a thick thread consistency, and pour into the mold. Soak the raisins in the rum. Remove the crusts from the slices of bread and butter them on one side. Beat the eggs and mix with the milk, the raisins and the rum. Add the syrup, making sure the mold is completely greased. Place the bread in the mold with the buttered side down. Pour a little mixture over the bread, and place another layer of bread in the mold. Repeat until finished. Leave for 30 minutes in order that the bread should become well saturated. Preheat the oven, place the mold inside and double boil for 25 minutes at a high temperature. Let cool in the mold.
