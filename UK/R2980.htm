Chestnut Jam

   FOR APPROXIMATELY 1000 cc

1 kg of peeled chestnuts.

1 kg of sugar.

3 tablespoons of rum.

Peel the chestnuts, only removing the outer shell. Cook in abundant water until tender. Drain them, and when they cool remove the inside husk. Weigh them, and for each kilogram of chestnuts make a syrup to a thread consistency with 1 kg. of sugar and 500 cc of water. When the syrup is of the right thickness add the chestnuts and cook on a low flame, while stirring carefully in order not to break them. The chestnuts will be just right when you can easily pass a pin through them. Remove from the syrup and place in the jars. Boil the remaining syrup with two or three tablespoons of rum and pour over the chestnuts.
