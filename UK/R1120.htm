Pork chops with White Sauce

   FOR 6 SERVINGS

6 pork chops

6 cloves of garlic

1 lemon

Salt

1/2 l of milk

1 tablespoon of cornstarch

2 eggs

3 tablespoons of breadcrumbs

250 cc of oil

1 beef bouillon cube

Make a paste with the garlic, the lemon juice and the salt. Coat the pork chops on both sides, put on a plate, cover and let stand for at least one hour. Prepare a white sauce with the milk and the cornstarch. Dissolve the cornstarch in cold milk and cook while stirring with a whisk. When it comes to a boil add the beef bouillon cube. Keep stirring until it thickens. Let cool. Fry the pork chops for 3 minutes on each side. Place on absorbent paper. Coat with a thick layer of white sauce on each side. Heat the oil. Beat the eggs, as if for an omelette. Dip the pork chops into the beaten eggs and then the breadcrumbs, pressing well. Fry in very hot oil. Place on absorbent paper.
